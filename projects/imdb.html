IMDb Ratings Analysis

What actually moves an audience rating? I explored a 5,273-film IMDb dataset to find out.

Role
Solo analysis
Tools
R, ggplot2, Tidyverse
Year
2023
Scope
5,273 films · 27 features

Selected visualizations from the analysis will be embedded here. Want the full write-up or code? Get in touch.

Context

I set out to understand which factors correlate with how audiences rate films. Starting from a raw IMDb dataset of 5,273 movies and 27 features, I filtered for the variables most likely to carry signal while deliberately retaining outliers, since extreme films often reveal the clearest trends.

Approach

Working in R with the Tidyverse, I cleaned and reshaped the data, then built six visualizations with ggplot2 to examine distributions in audience ratings over time, runtime, box-office performance, and rating system. Each chart tested a specific hypothesis about what correlates with higher ratings.

Outcome

The analysis surfaced clear relationships between rating and factors like runtime and release era, and I packaged the findings into a structured presentation that walked a non-technical audience through the trends and what they implied about viewer preferences.

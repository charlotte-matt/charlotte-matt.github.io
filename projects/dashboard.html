Context

Retailers buy inventory months ahead, often leaning on what's trending online. But does search interest actually predict what sells? This dashboard tests that assumption by pairing Google Trends color-search data with real H&M sales records from 2018–2020 across twelve garment categories.

What it shows

It moves from search behavior to actual sales to a forecast-accuracy scatter plot, where a reference line marks perfect prediction — points above it beat their search demand, points below overstated it. A color × category scorecard then breaks performance down product by product, ending in strategic takeaways for future buying decisions.

Why it matters

It's a compact example of turning two messy public datasets into a decision tool — connecting what people say they want with what they actually buy, the kind of question that drives real merchandising and marketing choices.
